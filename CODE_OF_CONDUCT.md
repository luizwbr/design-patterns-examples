# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our design patterns educational community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community focused on learning and sharing knowledge about design patterns.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our community include:

* Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people
* Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
* Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback on code implementations
* Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes, and learning from the experience
* Focusing on what is best not just for us as individuals, but for the overall community
* Helping others learn and understand design patterns across different programming languages
* Providing clear, educational examples and explanations

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

* The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind
* Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
* Public or private harassment
* Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email address, without their explicit permission
* Submitting code that violates copyrights or licenses
* Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional or educational setting

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned to this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including but not limited to the repository, issues, pull requests, discussions, and any public spaces where an individual is representing the project or its community.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the community leaders responsible for enforcement. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

All community leaders are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident.

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

This design patterns educational project is dedicated to fostering a collaborative and respectful learning environment. We welcome contributors of all skill levels and backgrounds who share our passion for software design and education.

## Expected Behavior

We encourage all participants to:

- **Be Respectful**: Treat everyone with courtesy and professionalism
- **Be Collaborative**: Share knowledge and help others understand design patterns
- **Be Constructive**: Offer helpful feedback on implementations and documentation
- **Be Patient**: Remember that people have varying levels of experience
- **Be Open-Minded**: Consider different approaches to implementing patterns across languages
- **Be Honest**: Acknowledge mistakes and learn from them
- **Give Credit**: Respect intellectual property and cite sources appropriately

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following actions are not tolerated:

- Harassment, discrimination, or intimidation in any form
- Offensive, derogatory, or disrespectful language
- Personal attacks or inflammatory comments
- Sharing private information without consent
- Plagiarism or copyright violations
- Spam or off-topic discussions
- Any conduct that disrupts the educational mission of this project

## Implementation Standards

When contributing code:

- Follow the language-specific guidelines provided
- Ensure implementations are educational and well-documented
- Test your code before submitting
- Respect the existing project structure
- Provide clear commit messages and pull request descriptions

## Reporting Issues

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:

1. Document the incident with relevant details
2. Report to project maintainers through appropriate channels
3. All reports will be handled with discretion and confidentiality

## Consequences

Violations may result in:

- **First occurrence**: Written warning and guidance
- **Repeated violations**: Temporary restriction from contributing
- **Serious violations**: Permanent removal from the project

Project maintainers reserve the right to determine appropriate responses based on severity and context.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project interactions, including repositories, issues, pull requests, discussions, and external communications representing the project.

## Acknowledgment

By participating in this project, you agree to abide by these guidelines and contribute to a positive learning environment.
